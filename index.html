Installation

ATLauncher

The ATLauncher is a third-party minecraft launcher that lets you easily assemble and launch as many different modpacks for as many different versions of minecraft you'd like.
The ATLauncher is the recommended way of installing this modpack. The following instructions will walk you through how to perform a manual install of the pack, however a fully compiled list of mods should be available for download soon.

ATLauncher Quick Installation (Recommended)

  • Install the ATLauncher

  • Install the Modpack

  • Start the launcher and log in with your minecraft account

  • Click "Vanilla Packs" in the right hotbar, find "Vanilla minecraft with Forge" and create a new instance. Image

  • Name the instance whatever you'd like, and choose minecraft version 1.12.2. Image

  • Click "install" and wait for it to finish.

  • Click on "instances" on the right and find your new minecraft instance. Image

  • Cick on "Open folder" under your game instance.

  • Copy all the files from the mod pack into to folder.

  • You're done! Hit "play" and your minecraft will launch. (Do NOT update the game, this will update to the lastest minecraft version. We want to stay on 1.12.2) I recommend having a quick look through your keybindings as all the mods you've downloaded will have some conflicting keys.

ATLauncher Manual Installation

  • Install the ATLauncher

  • Start the launcher and log in with your minecraft account

  • Click "Vanilla Packs" in the right hotbar, find "Vanilla minecraft with Forge" and create a new instance. Image

  • Name the instance whatever you'd like, and choose minecraft version 1.12.2. Image

  • Click "install" and wait for it to finish.

  • Click on "instances" on the right and find your new minecraft instance. Image

  • Cick on "add mods" under your game instance. This will open a new window where you can search for and easily install all the mods in this modlist. Image

  • Search for and add all the mods below. If a mod requires a dependency, don't forget to add it as well! (Dependencies are listed further down just in case).

  • You're done! Hit "play" and your minecraft will launch. (Do NOT update the game, this will update to the lastest minecraft version. We want to stay on 1.12.2) I recommend having a quick look through your keybindings as all the mods you've downloaded will have some conflicting keys.

Manual Installation

  • Install Minecraft Forge v.14.23.5.2854 for Minecraft 1.12.2

  • Visit the curseforge page for the mods below and download each mod. (Clicking on the mod title will bring you to that mod's page)

  • Install all mods to the mods folder within the "forge" profile of your Minecraft installation folder.

Modlist

Magic Mods

Botania

Botania is a tech mod themed around natural magic. The main concept is to create magical flowers and devices utilizing the power of the earth, in the form of Mana.

Thaumcraft

Thaumaturgy is the capability of a magician to work miracles. A practitioner of thaumaturgy is a thaumaturge, thaumaturgist or miracle worker. This is what this mod is all about - drawing magic from physical objects in the form of Essentia and reshaping it to perform miracles.

Blood Magic

Blood Magic is an arcane art that is practiced by mages who attempt to gather a vast amount of power through utilizing a forbidden material: blood. Even though it does grant a huge amount of power, every single action that is performed with this volatile magic can prove deadly. You have been warned.

Astral Sorcery

Astral Sorcery is a magic mod focused around harnessing the powers of starlight and the constellations. Explore and discover the world and the sky above you; focus starlight to your will, strengthening yourself or enhancing the world around you.

Roots 3

Roots is a mod about discovering the natural, wild magic of the world. There are points where the Fey world can bleed over into our world, and these can be taken advantage of for crafting, for rituals, for spells, and maybe even more! Discover forgotten herbs, rituals & spells that bring tremendous power and utility.

Dimension Mods

The Twilight Forest

Twilight Forest is a dimension exploration mod focused on adventure that will take you on a journey meeting strange creatures, exploring dungeons, and much more than able to be listed, but here are some of the major features:

Fully fledged dungeons.
Diverse boss battles with actual mechanics.
Items and loot with unique traits and functionality.

Block/Item Mods

Tinker's Construct

Tinkers' Construct is a mod about putting tools together in a wide variety of ways, then modifying them until they turn into something else. The tools never disappear and can be named and changed to your heart's desire. Once you make them, they're yours forever. Many different materials can be used to make your tools.

Construct's Armory

Construct's Armory is an add-on mod for Tinkers' Construct that adds four armor items: Helmet, Chestplate, Leggings, and Boots. These armor types can be customized just like the tools from Tinkers' Construct, allowing you to make different armor parts out of different materials in order to form a completely custom armor item.

Rustic

Rustic is small-ish mod that mainly adds medieval-themed content to the game. It focuses on decoration and agriculture for now, but it branches out a bit as well.

Rustic Thaumaturgy

A Cross-Compatability mod that adds Rustic furniture crafted with Thaumcraft wood, as well as aspects to Rustic's items.

Rustic BOP Woods

An addon for Rustic and Biomes O Plenty that adds tables and chairs for all the BOP wood types.

Bibliocraft

BiblioCraft is a storage and organizational mod that began as a single bookcase and has expanded to so much more.

Pam's HarvestCraft

Pam's HarvestCraft is an ever growing mod for Minecraft that currently adds (as of MC version 1.12.2):

80 new crops (including cotton for string)
6 garden bushes to find and harvest for crops (pick up bushes or let them spread like mushrooms)
50 fruit or item bearing trees
19 new fish (catch with normal fishing rod)
Simple Beekeeping (including candle blocks, wax/honey blocks, hardened leather armor, wax as fuel)
1400+ new items and foods

Just Enough HarvestCraft

A Minecraft Forge mod that adds Just Enough Items integration for Pam's HarvestCraft.

Wearable Backpacks

.. is a Minecraft mod which adds backpacks.

Trinkets and Baubles

A Collection of Trinkets and Baubles by XzeroAir, Formerly known as "First_Rain"

Chisel

Chisel adds a huge variety of decorative blocks to the game. The mod is very useful for people who like the construction aspect of Minecraft. Access to new blocks is provided mostly through one tool, the chisel. The iron chisel is created with a iron ingot and a stick in a diagonal pattern.

Cathedral

This mod provides extra blocks with a cathedral theme. Many of which are chiselable.

Comforts

Comforts is a mod that adds sleeping bags and hammocks to Minecraft. These items can be used just like beds except they do not set a player's spawn point on use, safely preserving a player's spawn point at their home base.

Mystical World

Mystical World is a content mod that provides the backbone for projects like Roots, with more to come! It adds many creatures, as well as metals & gems (copper, silver & amethyst, currently), and other mechanics that try to stay as close to vanilla as possible while still providing interesting and unique alternatives.

Quark

Quark is a fully modular mod with the objective of improving the vanilla gameplay experience.

AstikorCarts

Adds horse-drawn carts.

Akashic Tome

"The book of books!" Combine all the guide/tutorial books from different mods into just one, so you don't fill half your inventory with books.

Client-Side Mods (Optional)

These mods are entirely client-side, and are mostly just visual changes or QOL utilities. If you don't want 'em, skip 'em!

Optifine

A VERY highly recommended mod, Optifine provides many more options for video and graphics settings, adds support for things like shaders and natural/emmissive textures, and much more. If you're struggling for FPS, chances are Optifine can help.

Note: Optifine's rendering changes means that some guide books might have their text go off-page (or even off-screen). Though this can make things slightly more difficult when trying to learn certain mods, Optifine is still recommended as the benefits it provides can often outweigh these negatives.

Better Foliage

Better Foliage is a mod that will make your Minecraft worlds more impressive, mainly by improving how vegetation looks.

Controlling

Adds the ability to search for keybinds using their name in the KeyBinding menu, this allows players to easily find a key binding in the menu.
Adds a button that will only show KeyBindings that conflict with each other, which makes it easier to fix them.

Inventory Tweaks

This client mod allows you to easily manage your inventory, in both single player and multiplayer. It works out of the box, and will make you gain a massive amount of time!

It automatically replaces your tools as they break, and item stacks once emptied
It sorts your inventory and your chests in a single click/keystroke
It provides lots of powerful shortcuts to move items around
It allows you to customize entirely the way your inventory is sorted, thanks to clever configuration files

Mouse Tweaks

Mouse Tweaks replaces the standard RMB dragging mechanic, adds two new LMB dragging mechanics and an ability to quickly move items with the scroll wheel. Everything can be enabled and disabled in the configuration file or in Forge's mod options menu.

Light Level Overlay Reloaded

This is a mod visualizes light levels on nearby blocks.

Sound Reloader

Reload Minecraft's sound system without having to relaunch your game. (Useful if you need to plug in some headphones or something mid-game)

Resource Packs/Shaders

Completely optional, these are simply my personal recommendations, and what I'll be using while playing. Feel free to use your preferred resource packs/shaders, or none at all!

Unity Texture Pack

The Unity resource pack follows the traditional Minecraft aesthetics but adds subtle depth and more natural colors. It has support for many mods out-of-the-box, and will fit nicely with any that it doesn't support.

Note: on Unity's curseforge page, the author states that Unity is now modular. This isn't true for 1.12. Simply install the texture pack (v 1.6.4) and you're good!

BSL Shaders

While most shaders look good, and are often up to personal preference, I find that many shaders can conflict with mods. Especially with multiple aspects of Astral Sorcery. BSL is the only shader that I've found to work reliably, and it looks quite good too.

Note: Running any shader requires Optifine.

Note: Many shaders, including BSL, seem to conflict with Better Foliage, and will spam the chat with opengl errors. The error seems to have no effect on your game's stability, performance, or visual fidelity. If they're annoying, there's an option to "disable opengl errors" in Optifine.

Note: If using a shader that adds it's own sky, be sure to add dimension 0 to Astral Sorcery's weak sky rendering options under '\config\astralsorcery.cfg' (use ctrl+f and search for 'S:weakSkyRenders', and simpy add a 0 between the brackets like so)
